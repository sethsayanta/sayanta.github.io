Research Vision

At Virginia Tech’s Commonwealth Cyber Initiative (CCI) xG Testbed, my active portfolio balances physical zero-trust constraints with open standards implementation.

By optimizing deep cross-layer interactions within the O-RAN stack, my systems research aims to deliver actionable solutions for dynamic multi-tier spectrum access, zero-feedback interference isolation, and adaptive defensive posture against tactical intelligent jamming.

Research Projects Spotlight

6G Coexistence

DigiCaM: Feedback-Free Spectrum Sharing

Activating commercial terrestrial 6G deployments in the lower 12 GHz band is heavily constrained by the strict mandate to protect passive Fixed Satellite Service receive-only Earth Stations (FSS-ES) which neither transmit nor provide interference feedback. To circumvent this sensing bottleneck, we developed DigiCaM, a Context-Aware Digital Twin (CDT) framework that leverages site-specific 3D building geometry, 3GPP channel models, and real-time weather dynamics to infer interference without passive incumbent involvement. Utilizing a Recurrent Deep Reinforcement Learning (R-DRL) model structured with Lagrangian duality, extensive evaluation across a 33-MBS layout in Blacksburg, VA demonstrated up to an 84% spectral efficiency enhancement while maintaining strict compliance boundaries.

Spectrum Twin Architecture

POST: Programmable Open Spectrum Twin

Legacy spectrum-sharing architectures like SAS and TVWS suffer from high operational latencies, steep infrastructure sensing costs, and rigid worst-case interference assumptions that scale poorly in dynamic mid-band environments. This work introduces POST (Programmable Open Spectrum Twin), an advanced spectrum management framework that deeply integrates Open RAN (O-RAN) architectures with a proactive digital twin perception layer. By leveraging custom xApps and rApps within an Intelligent Spectrum Sensing Unit (ISSU), POST introduces real-time "what-if" policy testing simulations and predictive interference mitigation algorithms across complex multi-tier cellular infrastructures.

Network Security Emulation

Cognisseum: Cognitive Radios Facing Adversaries

While dynamic spectrum access and software-based controls bring flexible agility to modern radios, they also open new vulnerabilities to strategic, highly coordinated adversarial jamming and evasion attacks. We formulated algorithmic defense mechanisms utilizing cooperative game-theoretic optimization models that enable secondary user pairs to adaptively form secure, payload-maximizing coalitions against smart and naive adversaries. This security framework was fully implemented and benchmarked on the Colosseum wireless channel emulator—the world's largest wireless testbed—demonstrating optimal channel stabilization and robust defensive agility under real-time tactical jamming scenarios.

Colosseum Testbed Game Theory SDR Jamming Defense
mmWave Optimization

Directional Radio Allocation & Beamsteering Optimization

Harnessing directional mmWave frequencies requires continuous line-of-sight path alignment, introducing severe blockage issues and complex multi-user interference when forced to operate without continuous base station coordination. We proposed a role-based architectural classification framework that partitions directional radio nodes into Primary Antennas (PAs) and Secondary Antennas (SAs) running across a multi-phase Uplink/Downlink/PA-PA time-sharing layout. To avoid intractable computational complexity, we designed evolutionary algorithms and polynomial-time Recursive Random Search (RRS) heuristics to maximize sum-rate capacity, proving near-optimal performance across scalable networks of hundreds of nodes.
